🏆 Bug Hunter (15/49) — Catch a bug by swinging the net with perfect timing
Now that you have a net, you can catch bugs any time it isn't raining (once per day, day or night, with different bugs by time of day), and the bugs double as fishing bait. Bug-catching is mostly optional but is needed for a couple of quests, and it's the only source of Ryugu Stones until very late in the game — those combine with dungeon gemstones to trade for gear at Shiroku Pub.
Emperor Social Link established (Rank 1) — Kanji.
Watch the ripples from the bait — each fish has a unique pattern, so you can tell which is biting before it takes the hook: the Red Goldfish repeats a one-ripple pattern, the Genji Ayu a two-ripple pattern. You can reel in before a fish bites to retry without using a fishing attempt.

June 25 — Marukyu Striptease (Dungeon 2)
The Housewife's seed rotation adds Wall Paprika (resists Fire/Ice/Electric/Wind skills for 3 turns; 1 of each, ¥2,300, matures in 7 days) and Barrier Corn (lets one ally reflect a physical attack and one ally reflect a magical attack; 1 of each, ¥2,300, 8 days). You need to plant and harvest one Barrier Corn for a quest — check back daily if she's out. Wall Paprika is great for shielding a party member's elemental weakness on bosses, and reflect items can break certain later bosses.
You can choose your party for the first time. Use everyone fairly evenly rather than a core four — it gives you more SP to work with (swap out members who run low) and lets you match each member's strengths to a situation. You also need to use everyone to earn a later achievement, which requires hearing enough battle lines.
From today the Skill Level Up Card can appear in Shuffle Time. It randomly upgrades one skill on your equipped Persona — very powerful, but it adds RNG, so this guide avoids using it (and the fusion skills assume you do too). If you do use it, note which skills you changed so you can substitute them in the fusion guide. Saving periodically lets you reload an upgrade you don't want.
🏆 Fashion Plate (16/49) — Fight a battle while wearing a costume
Last chance for the Gentlemen's Tux — you must get one today before it becomes quest-needed. Also farm at least 6 Golden Cloths today (drop from Phantom Mages on Yukiko's Castle 5F–6F), as this is your last chance before they're quest-needed too.
You may also unlock a battle trophy around now for reaching 50 Sweep Bonuses — don't worry if it doesn't pop today, it comes easily by the end of the game.
🏆 Fill Your Hand (17/49) — Earn 50 Sweep Bonuses
Grab any new Personas you like from Shuffle Time, but don't delete the 8 you're carrying — they're needed for the upcoming fusions. You have two free slots to fill.
Steamy Bathhouse quest drops: Old Key (Bribed Fuzz, 1F–4F); 4x Curious Line (Cloudgirl Pot, 3F–5F & 8F–10F); Coal (Selfish Basalt, 5F–7F); 5x Thick Hides (Dancing Hand, 1F–2F & 5F–8F, including what you farmed earlier); Crooked Cross (Tranquil Idol, 3F–4F & 6F–8F); Reflecting Board (Crying Table, 8F–10F); Grand Horn (Grave Beetle, 8F–10F).
Optional (Artisian Apprentice): 8x Pure Iron Lump (Iron Dice, 1F–2F / 5F–7F / 9F–10F) and 10x Cloth Wings (Monopolizing Cupid, 1F–4F) — counting whatever you farmed here last time.
Curious Line isn't quest-required, but it unlocks a strong armor for the girls (high evasion plus Auto-Sukukaja, useful well into the endgame). Its source, the Cloudgirl Pot, only appears on rainy days — and rainy dungeon days also raise Golden Hand spawn rates.
Aim for level 31 with at least 80,000 yen before the next fusions and the 7F mini-boss. Farm Golden Hands for fast EXP and money; keep swapping floors to respawn them.
Items to grab as you work through Marukyu Striptease: 1x Silver Lump (Silver Dice, 5F–8F & 10F) and 3x Fashionable Dishes (Sky Balance, 9F–10F). Both are quest materials — don't sell them to Daidara by accident.
Optional farming for the Artisian Apprentice / Shiroku Pub weapons (needs extra money, and can be spread across later visits): 6x Steel Collar (Enslaved Beast, 3F–10F); 8x Tortoise Shell (Large Basalt, 3F–6F & 7F–8F); 6x Sharp Thorn (Mighty Cyclops, 7F–10F); 5x Fluorite (any mob in the Steamy Bathhouse or Marukyu Striptease, Kanji weapon); 5x Lepidolite (any mob in Marukyu Striptease, Yukiko weapon).
Register your Personas individually rather than using "register all" — it can overwrite a Persona you fused (and its added skills) with a Shuffle Time copy.

Personas you should be carrying now: Shiisaa (Hierophant), Gdon (Sun), Gurr (Jester), King Frost (Emperor), Narcissus (Aeon), Ose (Fool), Incubus (Devil), Ganga (Priestess), Principality (Justice), Ares (Chariot), Nigi Mitama (Temperance), and Ikusa (Hanged). These cover the arcana you'll work on this coming month, except Ose, which is purely a battle Persona — it has Power Charge (2.5x your next physical), Power Slash, and Atom Smasher, making it your go-to physical Persona. Get Ose to learn Auto-Sukukaja before leaving today, and get Ganga to learn Mediarama before the final boss.
Your Persona stock is full, so don't pick up any more from Shuffle Time for the rest of the dungeon.
Amorous Snake is weak to Ice but hits hard with physical attacks that inflict ailments, and it uses Stagnant Air to raise its ailment chance. Equip Ose on Yu (it resists physical and brings Rakunda, Power Charge, and Power Slash); have everyone attack physically and use Chie's Revolution for crits. The main danger is Panic landing on several members at once — Yukiko can clear it, but if she's hit you'll need an item or to switch Yu's Persona. When Yukiko isn't healing, she can chip in with Agilao.
Contrarian King (Level 17) drains Ice and nulls Light and Dark. Use Chie, Kanji, and Yukiko. Its Rampage hits very hard on Hard/Very Hard — soften it with Diamond Shields and/or Tarunda, keep Ose on Yu so physical attacks are resisted, and guard with Yukiko when she isn't healing. Have Chie use Revolution and farm crits and All-Out Attacks; Chie's Counter can reflect Rampage for big damage.
Intolerant Officer (Level 27) is weak to Wind and nulls Dark, but uses Wind and Ice attacks that Kanji and Yukiko are both weak to. Guard with Kanji and Yukiko to bait physical attacks instead (which Chie can Counter), and the Chie + Yukiko pairing can trigger Twin Dragons after an All-Out Attack. Knock it down with Kanji's Electric, then cancel the AOA to guard. Use Shiisaa on Yu (Rakunda, Zionga, resists Ice), and Ganga's Mediarama to heal without exposing Yukiko's weakness.
Before the Marukyu boss, recheck your Personas, that you have ~130k (including loot to sell), and that Ose has learned Auto-Sukukaja. Make sure you're carrying Personas of these arcana: Priestess, Jester, Sun, Chariot, Hierophant, Justice, Temperance, Aeon, Hanged, Emperor, and Devil.
Shadow Rise has no weakness and can use tier 1 and 2 single- and multi-target spells of every element plus Break spells and Mind Slice, so expect to get hit on weaknesses — just keep your HP up. She has low HP; use Rakunda, physical attacks, and Chie's Revolution. You can only take her to 50%, where she casts Supreme Insight and your attacks stop connecting — simply guard for the three turns until it ends.
Shadow Teddie drains Electric and resists Almighty. It's a long fight but very predictable. Keep Ganga's Mediarama ready (Yukiko's Media can't keep up on Hard/Very Hard); attack physically with Rakunda and skip Revolution (you can't crit him). Standard patterns: Mind Charge then Mabufula (guard with everyone on Mind Charge), Marakunda then Heat Wave, and Foolish Whisper to silence. Scripted attacks: around 75% HP he uses Ultra Charge then Nihil Hand (fully negated by guarding, but it insta-kills anyone dizzy); around 50% he uses Nulity Guidance (inflicts dizzy) then Ultra Charge and Nihil Hand — the dizzy wears off before it lands, so guard; around 25% he uses Nulity Guidance on two members, then Ultra Charge and Nihil Hand.
